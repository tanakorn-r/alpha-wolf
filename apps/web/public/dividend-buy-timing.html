Dividend buy timing for Thai SET and US stocks

Buying only for a dividend can be misleading because the share price normally adjusts around the ex-dividend date. AlphaWolf examines the complete historical cycle instead of treating yield as free money.

What Buy Timing studies

Ex-dividend rhythm

Historical ex-dividend and payment dates reveal whether the company has a stable annual or semiannual rhythm and how many observations are available. Seasonality is evidence, not a promise.

Post-ex price behavior

AlphaWolf measures how the stock behaved after previous ex-dividend dates: whether the price usually dipped, how deep the decline was, how long recovery took, and whether buying immediately or waiting historically produced a better entry.

Current-year alignment

The current year is displayed separately so investors can see whether recent month-to-date or year-to-date behavior still aligns with the longer historical pattern. For the current month, performance can accumulate through the latest available session.

Valuation and Agent character

Dividend seasonality alone does not force a buy. Ben may wait if the business is not a bargain. Sam may prioritize a safe, durable yield. Vera examines cash coverage, balance-sheet risk, and valuation. A trader or quant may require momentum or statistical confirmation.

How the monthly plan uses cash

A “25% of pool” buy uses 25% of all cash available at that month-end, not only one month's new contribution. Later buys can therefore use cash retained from earlier hold months plus accumulated dividends. Trims return proceeds to the cash pool, while character-specific holding rules prevent Agents from jumping in and out without discipline.

What Buy Timing cannot promise

A repeated historical pattern may break. Dividend amounts can change, payment dates can move, fundamentals can deteriorate, and a stock can remain expensive or continue falling after the ex-date. The tool should support a decision, not replace independent research.
